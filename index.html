Welcome to Ms. Clark's 3rd Grade Magnet Class!

I am delighted to have you and your child join my class this year. You're thinking: "Wait, did she say I just joinedher class?" Why yes, yes I did. We are all in this together! Since your child is a Magnet Technology student, by default you are as well. There is a bit of a learning curve and it takes a little adjustment to get used to the way my class is set up. However, you'll like that you can see what your child is working on daily (unless it's a paper and pencil assignment). The majority of classwork will be completed in an online environment. You will be provided with the username and password for each site your child will use throughout the course of the class.Feeling nervous? It's okay! I'm here to help.

Students will be required to work both independently and in groups. Students are expected to complete the work in the time that is allotted. All actvities, unless otherwise specified, are meant to be completed in the time that is given. Some students may need work in this area- and that's okay! I just need you to be supportive in my efforts to get your child working efficiently.

Speaking of efficiency.....what does that look like?

  • Having all supplies each day
  • Beginning the assignment when instructed to do so
  • In a group setting, only engaging in conversation and behavior that is related to the work
  • Actively attempting to solve problems BEFORE asking for help

I know it sounds like a lot, but don't worry- you and your baby can handle it!

A Few Rules...


So, there are a few rules to keep in mind for both parents and students. While the basics are laid out in the Student Handbook, there are some that aren't listed.
In our class, we will have rules that not only comply with school rules, but are specific to our class. Please ask your children to respect and follow the rules of the class and the school. It will help make our learning environment a better place for all. I know many people follow the mantra that "respect is not given- it is earned", but I would like for us to travel in the opposite direction. I want students to respect each other and all adults unless an issue truly causes a reason for disrepect. This will help our students judge situations better and generally will have students be a little more patient and understanding of each other's opinions.
There will be many discussions and situations where students will be forced to listen to someone else's beliefs or opinions and having a mindset of respect will help students through the process of listening responding appropriately.
Now, back to the rules......... I will generally either contact you via Class Dojo and/or send home a paper that needs to be signed and returned. Please return anything that requires a signature the next day.

Rules (in no particular order)

  • DeKalb County Student Handbook
  • Follow the Rules of All Adults in the Building
  • Respect Your Teacher (no talking back, walking out, refusal to follow instructions)
  • Be Nice to Your Classmates
  • Keep Hands, Feet, and All Objects to Yourself
  • Silence in the Halls
  • Use Your Walking Feet While in the Building
  • Bring All Supplies to School Each Day

That's it for the rules! I do suggest that you thoroughly read the rules in the Student Handbook because there are very detailed rules about other behaviors that all students must follow.
What about consequences? There aren't very many things that I can do. In the classroom my consequences are as follows: Loss of Class Dojo Points, Seat Moved, Special Privelege Revoked, Call/Note Home, Time Out, and No Recess. School consquences can be more severe: In School Suspension, Out of School Suspension, and Expulsion (most severe).

If you have any questions involving a situation with your child, please contact me via Class Dojo- this will give you the most rapid response.

Supplies

Last, but not least- actually other than behavior this is the biggest thing: SUPPLIES. Please ensure that your child has all school supplies each day. One of the things that delays our day the most, is students not having the things they need to complete tasks. I even suggest sending more than your child needs so that I have a cache of their individual supplies, should they forget something or run out.
While the supplies you purchase are for you child alone, I accept school supply donations. Please stick to the things on this list- and please do not send things that haven't been asked for- students usually get distracted and there are reasons I have not asked for certain supplies. Please also keep extra supplies at home for students to complete homework. Why? When students start bringing their school supplies home they get lost, broken, or they just forget to bring them back. Please do not expect your child to bring school supplies home during the week.
